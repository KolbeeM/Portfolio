01 — About

From the rec center to the regression line.

An unconventional path that turned out to be a feature, not a bug.

My career started where most analytics careers don't: managing people, programs, and operations. I earned my B.S. in Recreation Administration / Sports Management from Utah State University in 2025 — named to the 2024–2025 Dean's List — and spent those years watching gut-feel decisions wrestle with messy real-world data: staffing, attendance, cost overruns, member retention. One question kept surfacing: what does the data actually say?

That question pulled me into Python, SQL, and applied statistics. Now I'm back at USU pursuing a Master of Science in Data Analytics (expected May 2027), wrangling data in pandas, modeling behavior with scikit-learn, and translating findings into Tableau dashboards that non-technical stakeholders can actually use.

I work at the intersection of operations and analytics — equally comfortable in a staff meeting and a Jupyter notebook. I care about asking the right question first, choosing the simplest model that answers it, and being honest about what the data can and can't tell us.
